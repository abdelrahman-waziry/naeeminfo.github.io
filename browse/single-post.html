Real estate fuels top-line growth of 15.6% QoQ, 4.4% YoY on deliveries, construction spending

03 August 2015 19:20 AM

  • email
  • facebook
  • twitter


Emaar Properties (Emaar) 2Q15 P&L indicators are positive: income of AED1.179bn was up 16% YoY, 14.9% QoQ; ahead of our estimate of AED1.085bn - led mainly by top-line growth from the real estate business. Revenue came in at AED3.484bn; up 4.4% YoY, 15.6% QoQ – with real estate providing the required boost. Gross profit margin came in at 53.2%; down 2.84pps YoY, 1.2pps QoQ. While we await disclosure of the full set of financials to have a concrete view on the reasoning behind the margin decline; however, as per our assessment, the same could also be inferred as a result of recent changes to the accounting policy. SG&A was up 13.6% YoY, 21.4% QoQ; amounting to 21.5% of total sales during the quarter.

Change in accounting policy: Starting 1Q15, Emaar Properties had opted for early adoption of IFRS 15; allowing the firm to recognize revenues based on percentage of completion (instead of handovers or sales on delivery).

Segment wise, real estate contribution improved to 60% during the quarter (compared to 49% in 1Q15) on higher deliveries and construction spending. Revenue from the real estate business was AED2.107bn in 2Q15; up almost 41% QoQ. The strong growth was attributed to accelerated construction spending, which Emaar was able to record as revenue under the newly adopted IFRS 15.

With regards to the recurring portfolio, total recurring revenue grew 10% YoY to AED2.9bn. However, on a QoQ basis, the hospitality segment witnessed a correction of 5.2% YoY, 21.4% QoQ, with sales amounting to AED389m. Revenue from retail operations grew 10.8% YoY, while declining 3.8% QoQ to AED988m. Average occupancy at Address Hotels + Resorts was 87% in 1H15; almost flat YoY, but down from 92% during 1Q15.
